About the Project

Purpose, Background
& Objectives

This research investigates how investors perceive gold-backed digital assets — an emerging intersection of traditional commodity finance and blockchain technology. Understanding trust, awareness, and behavioural intent is critical for both regulators and market participants.


The study aims to identify key barriers to TER adoption, while assessing the comparative stability of gold versus volatile digital assets such as cryptocurrencies.

🎯

Research Aim

Understand investor awareness and trust toward gold-backed digital tokens.

📊

Methodology

Quantitative survey of retail and institutional investors across demographics.

🔍

Key Questions

How does familiarity with TER influence willingness to invest?

💡

Implications

Insights for policymakers, issuers, and financial educators in digital markets.

Market Analysis

Stability, Volatility
& Asset Behaviour

Understanding gold's historical stability compared to cryptocurrency volatility provides essential context for investor perception. Gold-backed tokens combine commodity security with the efficiency of digital settlement — appealing to risk-aware investors seeking diversification.

🥇

Gold

Low Volatility · Safe Haven

Gold has maintained its value across decades of economic turbulence. With annualised volatility typically below 15%, it serves as a hedge against inflation and currency risk — the foundation of gold-backed tokens.

Steady upward trend (indicative)

₿

Cryptocurrency

High Volatility · Speculative

Cryptocurrencies exhibit annualised volatility exceeding 60–80%, driven by speculation and regulatory uncertainty. While offering high return potential, this unpredictability deters conservative investors.

High volatility pattern (indicative)

🔗

TER Assets

Hybrid · Gold-Backed

Token-backed Exchange-traded Real assets bridge the gap — providing digital efficiency with commodity-grade stability. Their price is pegged to physical gold reserves, combining blockchain liquidity with tangible asset resilience.

📉

Risk Perception

Investor Sentiment

Survey data reveals 68% of respondents rated gold-backed tokens as "less risky" than pure crypto. However, 41% still expressed concern over counterparty risk — pointing to an education gap in the market.

Conclusion

Key Insights &
Implications

81% of aware investors expressed moderate to high trust in TER
57% expressed likelihood to invest in TER within 12 months
3× lower perceived volatility vs standalone crypto assets

"Gold-backed digital assets represent a compelling bridge between traditional commodity investment and the emerging digital economy — if trust and transparency keep pace."

This research demonstrates that while awareness of gold-backed digital assets remains limited, those who understand TER products show substantially higher investment intent and trust. The primary barriers are informational — not attitudinal. Regulatory clarity, transparent reporting of gold reserves, and accessible investor education are the three levers most likely to accelerate mainstream adoption.
