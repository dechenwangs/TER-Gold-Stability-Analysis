01
The Question

Why study TER?

On Bhutan's 118th National Day — December 17, 2025 — Gelephu Mindfulness City launched TER, the world's first sovereign gold-backed digital token. At the same moment, King Jigme Khesar pledged up to 10,000 Bitcoin (~$1B) to fund GMC. Bhutan wasn't just watching the digital economy — it was building one.

As a Data Practitioner at Bhutan Data Scientists Pvt. Ltd., a question emerged: can a gold-backed digital asset deliver greater stability than Bitcoin or Ethereum — and do Bhutanese investors trust it enough to adopt it?

"I didn't just study TER from the outside. I invested in it. This research combines academic method with personal financial commitment."

The project was shaped by Bhutan Data Scientists' three commandments: Own Your Time — deep focused research; Honor Your Word — data integrity and honest reporting; and Cultivate Independent Thought — going beyond the brief to ask better questions.

02
The Data

Five years of market evidence

To test TER's stability claim, the research needed a benchmark. Five years of historical price data (2020–2025) were pulled from Investing.com for gold, The Block for Bitcoin and Ethereum, and cross-validated against World Gold Council volatility benchmarks.

The window captured everything: the 2021 crypto boom, the brutal 2022 crash (−70% for BTC), the 2023 recovery, and the renewed 2024–2025 surge. Gold sailed through all of it — gradual, consistent, unshaken.

The annualised volatility calculation told the story in three numbers: Gold ~12%. Bitcoin ~72%. Ethereum ~82%. TER inherits gold's profile — making it approximately 6× more stable than Bitcoin.

03
The Survey

125 voices from Bhutan

Market data answers the stability question. But it can't answer the human one: do people in Bhutan actually trust TER? A structured quantitative survey was designed and administered to 125 respondents (18+), covering awareness, trust levels, investment behaviour, and adoption barriers.

85% showed moderate to complete trust — remarkable given that most had only basic awareness. Gold's cultural credibility was transferring to TER's digital form before mainstream adoption had even begun.

The hesitation data was equally revealing: the largest barrier wasn't price or technology — it was trust in digital assets as a category (33.33%). The 22.22% citing volatility risk is the exact gap TER's gold backing is designed to close.

04
The Findings

What the data actually said

The hypothesis was confirmed across both datasets. Gold's ~12% volatility vs Bitcoin's ~72% is a 6× gap — and TER inherits it. During the 2022 crash, Bitcoin fell over 70%. Gold barely moved. 88.24% of respondents preferred gold-backed digital assets for long-term value.

"Over 80% said they would probably or definitely hold TER — before mainstream adoption had even begun."

A key strategic insight: the 22.22% who prefer traditional gold over digital assets are TER's most natural audience — they just don't yet know TER IS gold. Education through DK Bank's channels could close this gap rapidly.

The research also produced four actionable recommendations: build digital asset trust through audits and RMA transparency; reframe TER explicitly as digital gold; use the volatility data to address risk perceptions; and run DK Bank education campaigns for the understanding gap.

Methodology & Data Sources

How the Volatility Was Calculated

Daily closing prices (2020–2025) were collected and converted to daily log returns. Annualised volatility = standard deviation of daily returns × √252 (trading days). Cross-validated against published World Gold Council benchmarks.

Investing.com · Gold The Block · BTC/ETH World Gold Council
Key Formula
σ_annual = σ_daily × √252

Where σ_daily =
std(log(P_t / P_t-1))
